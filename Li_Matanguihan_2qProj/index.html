The Depression and Bipolar Support Alliance (DBSA) is an organization stationed in America that focuses on mood disorders, which affect over 21 million Americans, account for over 50% of the nation's suicides every year, and cost $23 billion in lost workdays and other workplace losses.

DBSA offers peer-based, wellness-oriented support and empowering services and resources available when people need them, where they need them, and how they need to receive themâ€” online, in local support groups, in audio and video casts, or in printed materials distributed by DBSA, our chapters, and mental health care facilities across America.

Through their extensive resources and 450 support groups across the country, DBSA reaches millions of people each year with inperson and online peer support; current, readily understandable information about depression and bipolar disorder; and empowering tools focused on an integrated approach to wellness.

(DBSA, 2022)

Why This Advocacy?

As a person with bipolar disorder, I personally know the struggles and pain of everyday life, and seeing people go through the same thing I do hurts. I chose this advocacy so I could help the many struggling people in my own way, and so I could help educate many others on the many mood disorders that affect everyone's day-to-day life.
